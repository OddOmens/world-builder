tiers: |
  **Leather** — Entry level. Issued after a basic guild assessment.
  - Dungeon access: Floors 1–15
  - Jobs: Tier 1 only (local errands, basic creature removal, surface dungeon runs)
  - City entry: Free
  - Notes: Starting point for all adventurers. The license card is a stamped strip of leather.

  **Bronze** — Intermediate. Earned by completing a set number of Leather-tier jobs and passing a practical assessment.
  - Dungeon access: Floors 1–30
  - Jobs: Tier 1–2 (escort work, bounties on named creatures, multi-person contracts)
  - City entry: Priority over unlicensed travelers

  **Iron** — Advanced. For seasoned, battle-tested adventurers.
  - Dungeon access: Floors 1–50 (reaching the depth of the deepest known [[Portal Stone]] in most Great Dungeons)
  - Jobs: Tier 1–3 (dangerous creature hunts, dungeon recovery missions, city-contracted work)
  - Perks: Discount on guild hall services and equipment storage
  - Notes: Floors below 50 require special clearance even at this tier.

  **Steel** — Elite. Requires endorsement from existing Steel or Gold holders in addition to performance requirements.
  - Dungeon access: Floors 1–75
  - Jobs: Tier 1–4 (high-risk city defense contracts, named monster elimination, deep dungeon recovery)
  - Perks: Can officially register and lead a party under their license. Significant guild service discount. Name posted on the guild hall board.
  - Notes: The final 25 floors of any Great Dungeon still require Gold clearance.

  **Gold** — Master. Issued by the guild senior council. Cannot be fast-tracked. Fewer than a handful exist in the world at any given time.
  - Dungeon access: Unrestricted — all 100 floors of any Great Dungeon
  - Jobs: All tiers, including world-level contracts
  - Perks: Can take on apprentice adventurers. Guild housing in any city. Permanent name in the guild record books.
  - Notes: No Gold holder has yet cleared all 100 floors of any Great Dungeon.
