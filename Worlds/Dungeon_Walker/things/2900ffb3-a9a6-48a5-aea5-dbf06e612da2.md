tiers: |
  **Leather** — Entry level. The starting point for any trading career.
  - Trade rights: Single city only
  - Jobs: Tier 1 (local deliveries, market stall work, basic buy/sell contracts)
  - City entry: Discounted fees
  - Notes: Cannot legally move goods between cities at this tier.

  **Bronze** — Opens up regional trade routes and basic staffing.
  - Trade rights: Up to three nearby cities
  - Jobs: Tier 1–2 (inter-city delivery contracts, small bulk trades)
  - Perks: Can legally employ one licensed assistant. Reduced road toll fees on major trade routes.

  **Iron** — Established merchant status. Can run a permanent shop or trading post.
  - Trade rights: All cities across a wide region
  - Jobs: Tier 1–3 (bulk commodity contracts)
  - Perks: Can operate a permanent storefront or trading post. Can employ up to three licensed staff. Priority access to guild-posted trade contracts. Discount on guild hall storage.

  **Steel** — World-spanning trade rights. A significant economic player.
  - Trade rights: All cities and regions, including restricted and high-value goods categories
  - Jobs: Tier 1–4 (exclusive guild contracts)
  - Perks: Unlimited licensed staff. Major reduction in guild fees and road tolls. Name listed in the official merchant registry.
  - Notes: Certain uniquely rare goods still require Gold-tier clearance.

  **Gold** — The highest tier. Near-unlimited trade authority and political weight.
  - Trade rights: Unrestricted on all goods across the entire world
  - Jobs: All tiers
  - Perks: Can influence guild policy and vote on rule changes. Permanent exemption from most road tolls and city trade taxes. A Gold merchant's word carries near-legal weight in trade disputes.
  - Notes: Extremely difficult to earn. Gold merchants are highly visible and their dealings draw significant attention.
